GameChanger Tracks the Score. Team Season Captures the Story.

Both apps serve youth sports families, but in very different ways. Here's how they compare and when you might want both.

Two Apps, Two Different Jobs

GameChanger is a live game management tool. It tracks scores, stats, and play-by-play in real time. Coaches love it for running games. Parents love it for following along when they can't be there.

Team Season is a season journal. It captures the moments behind the scores — the car rides home, the rainy games, the things your kid said that you don't want to forget. Then it turns those entries into a hardcover photo book.

They don't compete. They complement each other.

When to Use Each App

Use GameChanger When

You need live scoring, real-time stats, or play-by-play tracking. When the coach needs a scorebook and remote family members want to follow along live.

Use Team Season When

You want to capture the personal moments — what your kid said in the car, the breakthrough at practice, the funny team dinner. The stuff that actually matters years later.

Use Both When

You want the complete picture. GameChanger runs the game. Team Season remembers the season. At the end, turn your journal into a book your family keeps forever.

Skip GameChanger When

Your league doesn't need live scoring, or you just want a simple way to remember the season without the complexity of stats and lineup management.

Why Families Choose Team Season

Most youth sports apps are built for coaches. Team Season is built for parents. It takes two minutes after each game to write down what happened, add a photo, and move on. No stats to track. No lineups to manage. Just a simple journal that becomes something your whole family will treasure.
