What to Include in Your Child's Season Recap Card

Feb 16, 2026 · 3 min read

The Essentials

You don't need to document every game. A great recap card captures the big picture of the season in a way that's quick to create and meaningful to look back on. Here's what to include.

Team Name and Season Year

This sounds obvious, but it's the detail most likely to get fuzzy over time. Was it the Lightning or the Thunder? Fall 2025 or spring 2026? Pin it down now while you remember.

Win-Loss Record

The overall record tells the story of the season at a glance. It doesn't need to be exact down to every tie - a close estimate is better than nothing.

Position Played

Kids move around a lot in youth sports. Noting their primary position or role for the season helps track how it evolved over the years.

Standout Stats

Points, assists, saves, times, personal bests - whatever matters for their role. A scorer's card looks different from a defender's card, and that's the point. If your kid played defense all season and didn't score, that's fine. Note the plays and moments that mattered.

Coach's Name

Coaches rotate, volunteer dads come and go. Your kid will want to remember who coached them, especially the ones who made an impact.

Tournament Results

If the team played in any tournaments, note the results. Even a first-round exit is part of the story.

The Details That Make It Special

Favorite Moment or Memory Quote

This is the best part. Ask your kid: "What do you remember most from this season?"

Their answer will surprise you. It probably won't be the game-winning play. It might be the time the whole team went to get pizza after a loss, or the inside joke they had with their teammate during warmups. That answer, in their own words, is worth more than any stat line.

Best Action Photo and Team Photo

One great action shot of your player and one team photo. That's all you need. Don't spend an hour searching for the perfect image - pick the one that feels right and move on.

Don't Overthink It

Here's the most important thing: even partial information makes a great card. You don't need every stat, every detail, every photo. A card with just the team name, record, and one good photo is better than no card at all.

The goal isn't perfection. It's preservation. Get the basics down now, and your future self will thank you.
