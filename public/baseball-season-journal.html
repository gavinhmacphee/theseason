Baseball Seasons Are Long. Memories Are Short.

A Little League season is 20 games. Travel ball is closer to 50. Add in tournaments, double-headers, and all-stars, and your kid might play 60 to 70 games between March and August.

You sit in the bleachers for every one. You cheer every hit, groan at every called third strike, and drive home talking about what happened. But by fall, the games blur together. Which tournament was the one where they hit the walk-off? What was the coach's lineup change that worked? Who was pitching in that game where it rained?

You were there. But the details are already gone.

Write It Down on the Drive Home

Team Season is built for the car ride after the game. Open the app, tap "New Entry," and write what happened while it's fresh. The score. Who pitched. What your kid did at the plate. Takes two minutes.

You don't need to write an essay. "7-4 W. Jake went 2-for-3 with a double in the gap. Coach had him batting cleanup for the first time." That's a page in the book.

What a Baseball Season Book Looks Like

Every journal entry becomes a page in the book. Games show the score, the opponent, and your notes. Practices capture what the coaches worked on. Moments hold the things that wouldn't fit in a box score.

  • Game pages — 7-4 W vs Marlborough, your notes, your photos from behind the backstop
  • Practice pages — the new swing adjustment, batting cage sessions, fielding drills
  • Tournament pages — the whole weekend, bracket play, the heat, the hotel
  • Moment pages — team dinners, sunflower seeds in the dugout, the walk to the car
  • Season summary — win-loss record, total games played, date range
