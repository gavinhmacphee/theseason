Why Most Sports Memories Disappear

Your kid plays eight months of soccer. Dozens of games, hundreds of practices, a few tournaments. You're there for most of it. You take some photos. Maybe a video or two.

Then the season ends. And within a few months, the details start to blur. Which game was the rainy one? What did the coach say that finally clicked? What was the score the day your kid scored from the halfway line?

You remember the big moments. But the little ones — the ones that actually mattered — slip away.

The Problem with Photo Albums and Scrapbooks

Some parents try to keep a scrapbook or fill a photo album at the end of the season. But that means sitting down after the season is over and trying to piece it all together from memory. Half the details are already gone.

The parents who actually succeed at capturing the season are the ones who write things down as they happen — on the car ride home, at the kitchen table that night. Two minutes while it's still fresh.
